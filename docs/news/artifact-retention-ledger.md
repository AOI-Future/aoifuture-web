# AOIFUTURE News artifact retention ledger

This ledger applies to Phase 1 contract artifacts. It separates AOIFUTURE's public editorial record from private operational evidence and from source material AOIFUTURE must not archive. The owner named here is an accountable role; access remains least-privileged.

| Artifact class | Status | Purpose | Owner | Deletion condition | Deletion/readback verification |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Published Edition manifest | Public, long-lived | Preserve the finite dated editorial snapshot and its minimal Signal records | AOIFUTURE editor | Keep while AOIFUTURE News operates; remove or redact only for a documented legal, privacy, safety, or rights obligation | Dated URL and repository manifest readback; correction/removal record remains when safe and lawful |
| Published Signal fields | Public, long-lived | Preserve source identity, concise source fact, AOI note, caveat, topics, role, and verification state without mirroring a source body | AOIFUTURE editor | Same as its published Edition; withdrawal removes it from current placement, not automatically from history | Historical Edition and ID-index readback show the Signal or documented redaction |
| Active Context and revision history | Public, long-lived | Show the current editorial view and attributable path of revisions | AOIFUTURE editor | Keep while AOIFUTURE News operates, subject to documented legal/privacy/safety removal | Context route and manifest readback; immutable-prefix validation against the preceding version |
| Correction, supersession, withdrawal, and source-unavailable state | Public, long-lived | Prevent silent rewriting and distinguish historical from current claims | AOIFUTURE editor | Keep with the affected public record | Validator checks state semantics; dated/public readback shows the visible state or correction note |
| Editorial method and material policy revisions | Public, long-lived | Explain selection, correction, source, and retention rules | AOIFUTURE editor | Supersede visibly; retain material prior policy while it is needed to interpret publications made under it | Public method-page and repository-history readback |

| Reader-level behavioral profile | Prohibited | Not required for ranking, Detour, or retrospective navigation | Privacy owner | Do not create | Analytics/configuration review confirms no stable reader profile or free-text/source URL payload |

## Retention rules enforced at the boundary

- Public schemas use closed objects. Unknown fields, copied bodies, private notes, scores, paths, credentials, prompts, and wrapper fields cannot hitchhike into output.
- The importer writes only `editions/*.json` and `contexts/*.json`. It never writes receipts or previous-state manifests.
- A receipt is valid only when one approved record matches the Signal ID, normalized source URL, source kind, and a usable claim locator.
- Deleting a private receipt makes that Signal ineligible for a fresh import/rebuild under this Phase 1 validator. Delete only after the public record and all supported rebuild obligations have ended.
- Retention expiry never authorizes copying an external body into the public record. A broken link leaves the minimal Signal and an availability state.
